# Stone & Standard v2.0.2

Celebrations now have a voice.

The audio plumbing has been sitting silently inside the celebration
overlay since v2.0.0 launch — toggle there, but no clips to play.
v2.0.2 wires in five voice clips Oak recorded on launch day,
mapped to the moment each one belongs to.

## What plays when

- **Throws PR** — randomly picks between **"Big throw!"** and
  **"See it, send it!"** every time. You won't hear the same one
  back-to-back across a long session.
- **Throws Goal achieved** — **"That's the practicing showing!"**
- **Height events** (Sheaf Toss, Weight for Height) — **"Up and
  over!"** plays on any PR or Goal in those events, regardless of
  what else fires.
- **Awesome Day capstone** (a session that hits two or more
  milestones at once) — **"A plan comes together."**

Lift PRs and Lift Goals stay silent for now — the gym side of the
app shapes differently than the throws side, and a voice clip in
that context can land wrong. Worth its own design pass when you
want it.

## Multi-milestone session — what you hear

A big session that hits a PR plus a Goal plus an Awesome Day
doesn't dump every clip back-to-back. The pattern is:

- First card in the queue plays its voice clip.
- Middle cards stay silent so the visual cards land cleanly.
- The Awesome Day capstone always plays at the end.

So a five-milestone session sounds like *"opening clip → quiet
visuals → closing capstone."* Less is more.

## Sound stays off by default

Same as it has since v2.0.0 — sound is OFF by default. The audio
toggle inside the celebration overlay is the one place that turns
it on. Your preference sticks across sessions.

## Replays stay silent

Opening a past session via **View Celebrations** replays the
visual cards without firing the voice clips. The clips are a
moment-of-achievement thing, not a flip-through-the-archive thing.

## Behind the scenes

- 386-test suite (up from 378 with the new audio-mapping coverage).
- Real WAV files at uncompressed 44.1kHz/16-bit stereo — small
  files, clean playback, browser-native `Audio()` element so no
  library dependency.

## Migration notes

No data touch. Open the app, the voices are there. Same URL, same
data, audio toggle does its job when you're ready for it.
