# Stone & Standard v2.0

Highland Games Tracker is now **Stone & Standard**. The name pairs the
two shapes of the sport — the **stone** you throw for distance and the
**standard** you clear for height — and v2.0 is the release where the
app grows up around them.

Open it in a browser, log your throws, watch your PRs come to life as
ornate celebration cards. Still no account, still no database, still
your data in your browser.

## What's new

### PRs and Goals, kept separately

Every event now carries two reference marks: a **PR** (your best ever)
and a **Goal** (what you're chasing). Both optional, both
independent. A veteran returning from injury can keep their old PR
and set a recovery Goal alongside it. A novice can leave PRs blank
and just chase the Goal column. The Set page is yours to shape.

### Celebrations when you hit one

Save a session that breaks a PR or hits a Goal and a celebration
card fires. Throws PR cards land on an ornate Highland Games-themed
template — shield, parchment medallion with your event silhouette,
stone plaque, brass scroll, thistle-and-laurel framing, misty
mountain backdrop. Lift PRs, Goal achievements, and Awesome Day
capstones (when a single session hits multiple milestones) get their
own cards too.

Every card is built for sharing — square or 4:5 aspect ratio, Stone
& Standard wordmark anchored at the bottom. Screenshot one and send
it wherever feels right.

Replay any past session's celebrations from the Past Sessions list
via the **View Celebrations** affordance. The cards you see later
look identical to the cards you saw at save.

### Strength and Conditioning, your way

The lift list is no longer hard-coded. Add your own lifts — Back
Squat, Front Squat, Trap Bar Deadlift, Atlas Stone Lift, whatever's
in your program. Each carries a free-text protocol field (`1RM`,
`AMRAP 30s`, `3x5 at tempo`) and a unit dropdown with ten options
across four categories: weight (lb / kg), distance (mi / K / m / yd),
time, and count (reps / rounds / cal).

S&C is now **milestone-only**. Don't log every gym day — log the
ones worth marking. A 1RM test, an AMRAP PR attempt, a max-effort
day. Most days, leave it blank.

### Progress, against your own bar

The Progress page (which replaces v1's "See the Gap") shows your
best mark in a chosen window — last session, past 3 sessions, or
year-to-date — against your PR, as a percentage. Toggle between
Throws and Lifts views. Events without marks in the window show as
"no marks logged" — the page tells you what you've done, not what
you haven't.

### A profile that fits

First launch captures a small profile: name, gender, weight schedule,
class, and tier — all optional, all local, never synced. The class
dropdown adopts the **Broken Caber Adaptive Athletics** framework
(federation-neutral, freely adoptable) and includes the four BCAA
Para-Standing classes alongside the standard Open, Masters,
Lightweight, and Junior options. Masters tiers run from M40 through
M65+.

### Backups you control

A **Download Backup** button at the bottom of the Set page exports
your data as a JSON file to your downloads folder. **Restore from
Backup** brings it back. Move it to another device, archive it, send
it to yourself — your data, in your hands.

Backup imports accept the old `comeback-tracker` and
`highland-games-tracker` envelopes too. Anyone with a backup from
any prior version can restore it cleanly.

## Behind the scenes

- Storage schema bumped v1 → v2 with automatic migration on first
  load.
- 376-test suite covering the data layer, celebration system,
  migration paths, and adaptive-athlete coverage.
- Biome linting in the build loop.
- The pre-rename `highland-games-tracker-v1` storage namespace is
  retired automatically on first v2.0 load, with defense-in-depth
  guards that keep your real data intact.
- Adaptive coverage for all five throws implements — ten silhouettes
  covering both able-bodied and adaptive athlete positions.

## Migration notes

- **The app is now Stone & Standard.** If you bookmarked the old
  Highland Games Tracker page, the URL is unchanged — just the
  branding. Your data carries forward automatically on first load.
- **No account, no signup, no manual migration.** Open the app,
  your data is there.
